## Introduction

As a Solution Architect working in pre-sales, my day is full of meetings. We sit with clients, often for hours, before we even open a laptop. We ask many questions to understand the real infrastructure they already have and to capture all their actual requirements.

The technology is the easy part. Understanding what the client truly needs is the hard part. Only after we have a clear picture do we start pointing at services. The diagram below is a great example of how we map those requirements to the right resource.

## The Body: Reading the Azure Compute Decision Tree

![Azure Compute Decision Tree](/images/articles/azure-compute-decision-tree.png)

The flowchart starts with one critical question: **are you migrating an existing workload, or building something new?** This single fork changes the entire conversation.

**If migrating**, the next layer of questions matters:

- Can the workload be containerized? If yes, we move toward container services.
- Is it a VMware workload? Azure VMware Solution preserves the existing investment.
- Is it a commercial off-the-shelf app? Azure App Service often hosts it with minimal changes.
- None of the above? Azure Virtual Machines remain the safe, flexible default.

**If building new**, we ask deeper questions to land on the right service:

- Need full control over the OS and stack? **Azure Virtual Machines**.
- High-performance computing or batch jobs? **Azure Batch**.
- Short-lived, event-driven processes? **Azure Functions** (serverless).
- Managed web hosting without thinking about servers? **Azure App Service**.
- Need full container orchestration? Choose between **AKS** (full Kubernetes), **Azure Container Apps** (serverless containers), **Azure Container Instances** (simple isolated runs), or **Azure Red Hat OpenShift** (for OpenShift teams).

The diagram also splits services into two helpful groups: **container-exclusive** (built for containers like AKS, ACI, ARO) and **container-compatible** (services that can run containers but were not built for them, like Batch, Functions, App Service).

> **Important note:** this diagram covers **Azure compute services only**. AWS and Google Cloud have similar offerings, but the names and trade-offs differ. Azure Functions maps roughly to AWS Lambda, AKS maps to EKS or GKE, and App Service maps to Elastic Beanstalk or App Engine. The principles transfer; the specifics do not.

## Summary

In most cases, the client or the system architect on the other side of the table knows very little about the cloud. That is not a weakness — it is the reality. Cloud is a deep field, and most teams are experts in their business, not in cloud architecture.

This is where the Solution Architect becomes important. Your job is to be their **honest consultant**. Ask the right questions, listen carefully, and gain the knowledge needed to make the right decision on their behalf.

The diagram does not make the decision. **You do**. And you can only make a good decision when you have asked the right questions first.
